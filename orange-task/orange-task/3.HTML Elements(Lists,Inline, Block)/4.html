Car Engine

A car engine, often referred to as an internal combustion engine, is a complex machine designed to convert fuel into mechanical energy to propel a vehicle. At its core, the engine operates on the principles of combustion, where fuel and air are ignited to produce a high-pressure force that moves the pistons. This motion is transferred through a crankshaft, which ultimately drives the car's wheels. Modern engines come in various configurations, including inline, V-shaped, and boxer designs, each offering different advantages in terms of power, efficiency, and smoothness.
